04 // EXTENSIONS

Where this goes next.

EXT / 01

Path- & time-dependent payoffs

The genuinely hard, high-value trades need the whole path — exactly where classical MC is most expensive. Our register already holds every path in superposition: pricing the Asian is a one-scalar swap, f = S_T → f = S̄ — demonstrated end-to-end in §03, on simulator and real hardware. And term structures σ(t), r(t) are native — each tree step gets its own rotation angle θᵢ, at zero extra depth.

EXT / 02

Entangling different options

One asset = one path register, one qubit per step. Two correlated assets = two registers plus an entangling layer calibrated to their correlation ρ — the joint path measure, prepared as one state. Baskets, spreads and cross-asset risk would then be read out the same way: one multiplexed payoff rotation, one amplitude estimate — instead of a fresh Monte Carlo per instrument pair.

EXT / 03

Mapping to different domains

Nothing in the circuit knows about stocks. It estimates E[f(path)] for any process built from per-step binary events — insurance claim cascades, credit defaults, energy demand, failure chains. Changing domain means changing the payoff angles φₓ; the loader, the multiplexer and the O(1/ε) readout carry over unchanged.
