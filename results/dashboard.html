3 · Where the SOTA error comes from — truncation + linearisation, not QAE

The SOTA loads the continuous price law into an n_p-qubit price register (2n_p grid points), inside truncation bounds of ±3σ. Honest finding from the numbers below: adding price-register qubits refines the grid but does NOT shrink the total error here — because the dominant error is the ±3σ truncation (the call's fat upper tail is chopped off, a fixed ≈ −0.36 floor) plus the c_approx=0.25 rescaling-linearisation bias (≈ +0.18). The representational value the grid converges to is ~2.27, not Black–Scholes 2.64. The near-miss at n_p=3 is a coincidental cancellation of those two biases, which unwinds as n_p grows. SOTA can approach BS only by also widening the bounds and shrinking c_approx — more qubits/depth. Our exact binomial loading has no truncation and no linearisation (exact finite product measure) — the price register is the qubit cost our phase routes avoid entirely. 7 · Honesty notes (read before quoting any number)

  • No hardware noise. Every SIMULATED number here is noiseless (statevector / finite-shot ideal Sampler). Real-hardware (q50_fake) runs show ~15% error at M=1 — not in this dashboard.
  • Two references, on purpose. Our routes vs the exact tree; SOTA vs Black–Scholes. They load different distributions; the tree–BS gap is discretisation, not error.
  • The SOTA's residual error is loading-limited, not QAE-limited. With standard settings (±3σ bounds, c_approx=0.25) it is dominated by tail truncation (≈ −0.36) + payoff linearisation (≈ +0.18); these do not vanish by adding qubits. Tuning bounds/c_approx can close it — at extra cost. This is a fair, real property of oracle-loading a continuous law, not a strike against QAE.
  • QSVT carries a polynomial-approximation floor (degree-60 straddle polynomial near the payoff kink; the exact size is printed in its table row). It shrinks with degree and depends on the free design constants (A,B) — design-dependent, not fundamental.
  • Calibration is look-ahead-free. σ and S₀ use only closes on or before t₀; r is a fixed proxy chosen as of t₀. The out-of-sample section is the only place the model meets reality, and its single-path payoff row is illustrative by construction.
  • QNDM Fourier is O(1/ε²) in shots, like MC — its win is shallow depth & exact loading, not ε-scaling. Only QAE/QSVT give the O(1/ε) quadratic speed-up.
  • QAE query schedule saturates at small M — the honest empirical points sit below the theoretical line and are annotated as such.
  • Quantum advantage is asymptotic / fault-tolerant. This is a small-M simulation showing the principle & the engine; production speed-up needs hardware that does not yet exist.
