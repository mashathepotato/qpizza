# Challenge: Quantum Computing for Finance — OP Pohjola @ Junction Helsinki

## One-line
Use quantum computing to tackle the hardest problems in modern finance — show a
**genuinely quantum-native** approach with a clear problem, working PoC, and business case.

## What they want
- Identify a **concrete** financial problem (portfolio optimization, option pricing,
  or any related domain).
- Demonstrate how quantum algorithms could offer a **meaningful advantage** — not just
  "faster", but reshaping what is computationally feasible.
- NOT a production system. They want: a compelling quantum-native approach, clear problem
  formulation, and a working proof-of-concept.

## The key bar (read this twice)
> "We are looking for **originality above all**. Solutions that demonstrate a genuinely
> quantum-native approach to a meaningful financial problem, rather than **classical methods
> with a quantum label attached**."

This is the trap most teams fall into. Avoid "we ran QAOA on a 4-asset toy portfolio" with
no insight. Win by making the *quantum structure* essential to the formulation.

## Judging criteria (each 25%)
1. **Novelty & creativity of the quantum approach** — 25%
2. **Quality of the problem formulation** — 25%
3. **Technical depth of the implementation / simulation** — 25%
4. **Clarity of the business case** — 25%

Balanced weighting ⇒ a brilliant algorithm with a vague business case scores the same as a
clear business case with a shallow demo. We must hit **all four**.

## Three suggested avenues (from OP)
1. **Portfolio optimization** — QAOA or VQE for optimal portfolio construction.
2. **Option pricing** — Quantum Amplitude Estimation (QAE) for price estimation, or
   quantum generative NNs for volatility surfaces.
3. **Quantum ML for finance** — risk premia for asset pricing, anomaly detection on
   quantum hardware.

Teams are **encouraged to invent their own** problem + solution. Originality is rewarded.

## Prizes
